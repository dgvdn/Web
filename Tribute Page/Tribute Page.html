Hồ Chí Minh

The great master of the Vietnamese revolution

Here's a time line of Hồ Chí Minh's life:

  • 1890 - On May 19, Hồ Chí Minh was born in Kim Liên, Nam Đàn, Nghệ An, Annam, French Indochina.
  • 1907 - He received a French education, attending Collège Quốc học (lycée or secondary education) in Huế.
  • 1910 - Became A Schoolteacher
  • 1911 - He worked as a kitchen helper on a French steamer, the Amiral de Latouche-Tréville, using the alias Văn Ba. The steamer departed on 5 June 1911 and arrived in Marseille, France on 5 July 1911.
  • 1912 - While working as the cook's helper on a ship in 1912, Thành (Ho) traveled to the United States.
  • 1917 - Moved To Paris
  • 1920 - Quốc (Ho) became a representative to the Congress of Tours of the Socialist Party of France, voted for the Third International and was a founding member of the French Communist Party.
  • 1923 - Quốc (Ho) left Paris for Moscow carrying a passport with the name Chen Vang, a Chinese merchant, where he was employed by the Comintern, studied at the Communist University of the Toilers of the East
  • 1925 - He organized "Youth Education Classes" and occasionally gave socialist lectures to Vietnamese revolutionary young people living in Canton at the Whampoa Military Academy.
  • 1927 - After Chiang Kai-shek's 1927 anti-Communist coup, Quốc (Ho) left Canton again in April 1927 and returned to Moscow, spending part of the summer of 1927 recuperating from tuberculosis in Crimea before returning to Paris once more in November.
  • 1930 - Founded Communist Party of Vietnam
  • 1931 - He was arrested by British Colonial Authorities in Hong Kong
  • 1941 - Hồ Chí Minh returned to Vietnam to lead the Việt Minh independence movement.
  • 1945 - Became President
  • 1969 - Death From Heart Problems
